More Than Just a Code: A Day in the Life of an IT Student

Introduction

      Honestly, if I had known earlier that IT would be this intense, I probably would have thought about it longer. Not saying I regret it, but it really hits different. Before, I thought IT was just about computers—typing code, staring at screens, and that’s it. But now, as an IT student, every day feels like a small battle with stress, sleepless nights, pressure, and endless deadlines.

      This blog is just me sharing what a normal day in my IT life really looks like. No sugarcoating—just a real story.

Morning: Wake Up, Check the Phone, Panic Mode

      Most of the time, my day starts with checking my phone. Messenger, group chats, Canvas, and even the time—just to see if I’m already late or if I still have a chance to make it. Most days, I already assume I’m late, and that’s when the panic really kicks in.

      Every morning, I use my father’s motorcycle to go to school. The ride usually takes around 30 to 60 minutes. During the ride, my mind is already full of thoughts: unfinished tasks, upcoming deadlines, and whether I remembered to submit something or not.

      Sometimes the road is calm, sometimes traffic is bad, but either way, that travel time gives me space to think. By the time I arrive at school, I’m already a bit exhausted, but I remind myself that this is just another day I need to get through and learn from.

Afternoon: Code, Errors, and a Bit of an Existential Crisis

      When coding subjects start, this is the part where I sometimes stop and think, “Is this really for me?” There are days when I understand the lesson easily, and there are days when one small mistake in the code can keep me stuck for hours. It’s frustrating and draining.

      Sometimes I wonder if I’m just not good enough, but later on, I realized that this is actually normal. Everyone gets errors—even the good ones. The difference is they don’t quit right away.

Evening: Tired but Still Moving Forward

      At night, the exhaustion really hits. Sometimes I review lessons, sometimes I just stare at the screen and think about how I survived the day. But despite everything, there’s still a small sense of fulfillment.

      Even if the path was full of errors, at least I learned something.

Conclusion / Reflection

      The truth is, IT is not for people who give up easily. It’s not enough to just be good with computers—you need patience, resilience, and the courage to accept mistakes.

      Rest when you’re tired, complain if you need to, but don’t quit. One day, all these errors will be the reason you become stronger.

Lusanta, John Eugene R.
